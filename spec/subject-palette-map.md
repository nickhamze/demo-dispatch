# Subject-to-palette pairings

Each subject is assigned exactly one accent pair. Pairings are distributed so
any randomly-selected sequence of three illustrations uses at least two
distinct pairings, preventing visual monotony.

Pairing legend:

- **A** = terracotta `#B8533A` + ocean teal `#2F6F6A`
- **B** = mustard ochre `#D6A24E` + sage green `#7A8C6F`
- **C** = terracotta `#B8533A` + mustard ochre `#D6A24E`
- **D** = ocean teal `#2F6F6A` + sage green `#7A8C6F`

## Distribution accounting (after v2)

- A: 8 (v1) + 6 (v2) = 14
- B: 7 (v1) + 5 (v2) = 12
- C: 8 (v1) + 5 (v2) = 13
- D: 7 (v1) + 2 (v2) = 9

The distribution skews intentionally: A and C are over-weighted in v2
because the marketing pages they appear on (Shop, Press kit, project case
studies, Studio) carry the bulk of new visual surface, and these pairs
read most clearly at small card sizes. Pair B is concentrated in the
kitchen cluster on purpose — those subjects belong together visually.

No subject is reassigned without re-balancing.
