**Client:** A friend who runs a small restaurant two cities east, and her
mother, who has been writing the recipes down since 1971.

**Brief:** A small-press cookbook collecting forty of her mother's recipes.
Hand-set headings, generous margins, full-bleed photographs of the food.
A book the friend would want to give to her own daughter.

**Approach.** We worked through the recipes one weekend at a long kitchen
table, deciding which forty out of the seventy-three the mother had written.
We cooked twelve of them ourselves before we had any opinions about how the
book should look.

![A spread from the cookbook, showing a recipe and a full-bleed photograph
on the facing page.](cookbook-spread.webp)

The book is set in a single typeface family, contemporary, with old-style
numerals for the quantities. Headings are hand-set on the studio's press in
a contrasting display face and pasted into the master at the right angle to
look as though they were typed onto each page. The mother liked this very
much.

![Lemon cake, the recipe in the book, photographed at the studio.](lemon-cake.webp)

**Outcome.** Ninety-six pages, sewn binding, hard cover with a blind-debossed
spine. A run of three hundred. The friend gave the first copy to her mother
on a Tuesday afternoon and called us afterwards. The mother had read it
straight through, looking for what was missing. She said nothing was.

A few copies of the second printing are on the [Shop](/shop/) page when
they exist. The first printing is gone.
