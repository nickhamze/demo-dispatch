You will need one rectangular sheet of paper. A4, US Letter, or any
proportion close to root-two will work; the boat is more elegant on lighter
paper, but heavier paper floats longer.

## Steps

1. **Fold the paper in half along the short axis.** Crease the fold firmly
   with a thumbnail. The folded edge is now at the top.
2. **Fold the top corners down to meet at the centre line.** You should
   end up with a triangular shape on top of an unfolded strip at the
   bottom.
3. **Fold the unfolded bottom strip up on the front, then turn the model
   over and fold the matching strip up on the back.** The model is now a
   triangle with a flat base and two folded flaps.
4. **Open out the base and reshape the model into a square.** Bring the
   two pointed corners together at the bottom; the model collapses into a
   diamond.
5. **Fold the bottom point of the diamond up on the front and on the
   back.** You now have a smaller triangle. Open out the base again and
   collapse it into a smaller diamond, then pull the two top corners apart
   to form the boat.

![A finished paper boat in a shallow puddle.](paper-boat.webp)

The boat will float for somewhere between thirty seconds and three minutes,
depending on the paper and the water. It is improved, in my view, by being
launched with a small ceremony - a hand on the side of the puddle, a
short word, an unhurried release.
