Three bicycles, in chronological order, with brief notes on each.

## The first bicycle

A second-hand mixte frame, possibly French, possibly Italian, with five
gears and a rod brake on the front. It was painted, when I bought it, a
particular dark green that the previous owner had applied with what
looked like a household paintbrush. The brushstrokes were visible. I
liked them.

It was stolen, four months after I bought it, from outside a library
where I had locked it carefully to a railing that was, it turned out, not
attached to anything. The railing went too. I have always slightly hoped
that the thief took the railing with them.

## The second bicycle

A pragmatic, slightly ugly steel city bike, painted black, with a basket
on the front and a rack on the back. I owned it for nine years. It went
to work. It went to the supermarket. It went to the river. Once, fully
loaded, it carried two weeks' worth of holiday luggage to a campsite an
hour away and then carried us both home again. The frame was indestructible.
The components were not.

I gave it away when I moved cities. The friend I gave it to is, as far as
I know, still riding it.

## The third bicycle

A folding bike, partly because the new flat has no bike storage and
partly because I have always slightly fancied a folding bike. It folds in
about ten seconds. It rides surprisingly well. The wheels are small enough
that the ride is bumpy on cobbles, which fortunately the new city has very
few of.

It does not have a name. The previous two had names. I am not sure why
this one does not. Perhaps it will earn one in time.
