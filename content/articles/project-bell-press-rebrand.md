**Client:** Bell Press, a 70-year-old neighborhood print shop two streets
over.

**Brief:** A new wordmark, a small identity system, and a single two-color
letterpress card to announce the move to the new shopfront. Quietly modern.
No "rebrand" in the sense the word usually means. The old hand-painted sign
on the front window had to feel related, not replaced.

**Approach.** We spent a morning at the press with Bell himself, looking at
sixty years of his own composing-stick work. The wordmark we eventually drew
borrows the long descenders and the wide counter from his 1972 wedding
invitations and nothing else.

![A short stack of trial posters, two-color letterpress, drying on the
rack.](poster-stack.webp)

The card is two passes — a warm grey for the address block, a deep terracotta
for the wordmark. We proofed it twice and printed a hundred for the opening.

![Three proofs on rough cotton paper, printed two-color, terracotta and
warm grey.](proofs.webp)

**Outcome.** Bell hung the new sign on the new shopfront last Tuesday. The
mark looks like it has always been there, which is what Bell asked for and
what we hoped to do.

A small case study, by design. Print is in the
[Shop](/shop/) for collectors who want one of the trial posters. Larger work
for Bell is ongoing.
