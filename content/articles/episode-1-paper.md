The first episode of an occasional podcast about small things. Mira and I
sit at a kitchen table for forty-three minutes and talk about paper:
the surface, the sound, the smell, the way it ages, and why a particular
A4 sheet from a particular Italian mill has been sitting in a folder on
Mira's desk for nine years without ever being written on.

## Chapters

1. **(00:00)** Cold open: rain on a tin roof.
2. **(01:14)** Introductions and the paper on the desk.
3. **(06:32)** Why the surface of a paper is the most important thing about
   it, and what "tooth" means.
4. **(13:48)** The smell of paper, and why old paper smells different from
   new paper. (Hint: the lignin.)
5. **(21:15)** A short tangent about the sound of paper, and why some
   papers are noisier than others.
6. **(28:40)** The Italian mill, and the nine-year-old A4 sheet.
7. **(36:02)** Paper sizes and the square root of two.
8. **(40:55)** Closing thoughts and the next episode.
