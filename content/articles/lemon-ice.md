A two-ingredient recipe. Best made the day before you want to eat it.

## Ingredients

- 6 lemons (unwaxed if you can find them).
- 200g caster sugar (white).
- 600ml water.

The sugar and the water make a syrup. The lemons make the rest of it.

## Method

1. Zest two of the lemons. Set the zest aside.
2. Juice all six lemons. You should end up with about 250ml of juice.
3. Combine the sugar and water in a saucepan. Bring to a simmer, stirring
   only until the sugar dissolves. Add the zest. Take off the heat.
4. Let the syrup cool, then strain out the zest.
5. Stir in the lemon juice.
6. Pour the mixture into a shallow tray and put it in the freezer.
7. After 90 minutes, take it out and rake it through with a fork. Return
   to the freezer. Repeat every 90 minutes for the next four hours, or
   until the texture is the granular, snow-like consistency you want.

Serve in small, cold cups.

![Lemon ice served in a small porcelain teacup.](teacup.webp)

It is the right pudding for a Sunday afternoon at the end of summer, when
nobody wants anything heavy and there is still warmth in the garden. It is
also good - and this is admittedly slightly less seasonal - in February,
served alongside a single shot of cold espresso.

The recipe scales linearly. If you want twice as much, double everything.
The freezing time does not change.
