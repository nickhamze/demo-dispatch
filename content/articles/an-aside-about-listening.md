Rain on a tin roof, listened to from the warm side of an open window, is
one of the better short pieces of music available to most of us, free of
charge, on most evenings between October and March, in most parts of the
world.

It does not, of course, sound the same anywhere. The pitch depends on the
gauge of the metal. The rhythm depends on the pitch of the roof and the
strength of the wind. The dynamics depend on whether the rain is light,
heavy, or in the middle stages of becoming a storm. But the basic
composition - many small percussive impacts, very close together,
unpredictable in detail and absolutely predictable in character - is the
same everywhere.

I have nothing else to say about it. I just wanted to mention it.
