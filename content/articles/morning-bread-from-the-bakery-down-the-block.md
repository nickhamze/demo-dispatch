People ask, often enough that it is worth writing down: no, we do not bake
the bread ourselves. The bread comes from the bakery down the block, the
one with the blue door, run by a couple who get up earlier than I do and
who have been at it for nineteen years.

We tried baking it in the early days. It was fine. It was not as good as the
bread from the bakery down the block, and it tied up the small oven for
three hours every morning, and the oven is the only piece of equipment in
the kitchen we have a real argument with. So we decided we would rather pay
for good bread than make mediocre bread.

The loaves come over at six in the morning, four to start, more on Saturdays.
They are on the counter by seven. If you come early they are still warm.
Sliced and toasted with butter, with the lemon marmalade we put up in
February, with anything from the [Menu](/menu/) — they are the quiet center
of every morning here.

If you ever want to know whose bread you are eating, walk one block south
and look for the blue door. Tell them we sent you.
